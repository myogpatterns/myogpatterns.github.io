Materials

When selecting materials for sewing the Packlite Hat, especially for outdoor use, key factors include breathability, sun protection, and durability. This guide helps you choose fabrics and notions that balance comfort, functionality, and ease of sewing for activities like trail running, hiking, and other endurance sports.

Key Considerations

  • Breathability and Moisture-Wicking: Look for fabrics like ripstop nylon, taslan nylon, and polyester mesh that allow airflow and wick moisture away for optimal comfort during intense activities.
  • UPF Protection: Fabrics with a UPF rating block harmful UV rays. For outdoor sports, a UPF rating of 30-50+ is ideal for enhanced sun protection.
  • Water-Resistance (DWR Coating): Durable Water Repellent (DWR) coatings help shed light rain and moisture, making these fabrics excellent for unpredictable weather.

Fabric Recommendations

  • Supplex Nylon: Lightweight yet durable, Supplex nylon (aka taslan) is ideal for ultralight projects and has a matte finish that resists snags.
  • Ripstop Nylon: A classic choice for outdoor gear, ripstop offers strength and breathability. Versions with DWR coatings add extra water resistance.
  • Breathable Mesh: Perfect for side panels or the back portion of the hat, breathable mesh keeps you cool during high-output activities. Nylon or polyester mesh fabrics ideal.

Brim Material

3mm EVA Foam: Optimal for a pliable, packable brim that holds its shape even after being stowed. EVA foam is lightweight, durable, and easy to work with.

Sweatband Material

  • Performance Knit or Wicking Jersey: These soft, moisture-wicking, and quick-drying fabrics provide the best comfort for interior sweatbands, efficiently managing sweat during active use. They’re lightweight and breathable, making them ideal for ultralight, performance-focused designs.
  • Pre-Made Hat Sweatband: For a more traditional approach, pre-made hat sweatbands are a viable option. While they add some weight and can become less comfortable when saturated, they offer a consistent fit and structure. Pre-made bands are especially useful if you prefer a ready-to-go solution.

Back Closure Options

  • Webbing with Ladder Lock or Center Release Buckle: 1/2" lightweight webbing paired with a plastic ladder lock or center release buckle offers a secure and adjustable fit.
  • Shock Cord with Cord Lock: For a minimalist closure, shock cord combined with a cord lock and metal eyelet provides a stretchy and adaptable fit.

Pattern Difficulty

Sewing UL Fabrics

This pattern is rated for advanced beginners or intermediate sewists due to its technical requirements and precise construction. You'll need a zipper foot (presser foot) for sewing close to tight edges and a walking foot attachment can be a game-changer, especially when working with slippery or lightweight fabrics. A serger is strongly recommended for cleanly finishing interior seams, ensuring durability and a polished look.

This project offers a great opportunity to elevate your sewing skills by tackling curved seams, precision topstitching, and working with ultralight fabrics. The design is optimized for lightweight, breathable materials, which require extra attention to detail. If you’re new to sewing these types of fabrics, we recommend reading Mastering Techniques for Sewing Ultralight Apparel Fabrics for valuable tips on handling uncoated, technical textiles. Though this pattern is challenging, it's also highly rewarding, producing a versatile and functional hat that’s perfect for outdoor adventures.
